# motyl.dev Weekly #19: Week 27 (Jun 29 – Jul 5, 2026)

> A curated digest of what I found worth reading this week.

This week's picks orbit a single tension: the industry keeps racing ahead on agentic tooling while most organizations haven't even flipped the AI switch yet. There's conference gossip from the AI Engineer World's Fair, a healthy dose of skepticism about performative AI adoption, and some very practical notes on running coding agents without burning money.

## 🤖 AI

**[AIEWF Daily Dispatch: The great loops debate and the state of AI engineering](https://www.latent.space/p/aiewf-daily-dispatch-locomotives)**
Latent Space's dispatch from the AI Engineer World's Fair, centered on the "great loops debate", how much of an agent's control flow should live in the model versus the harness. A good pulse check on where AI engineering as a discipline actually stands right now.

**[Own The Outer Loop](https://addyo.substack.com/p/own-the-outer-loop)**
Addy Osmani argues that as AI takes over the inner loop of writing code, the engineer's leverage moves to the outer loop: framing problems, reviewing, integrating, and owning outcomes. Short, sharp, and worth internalizing before your next planning cycle.

**[Agentic Harness System Design: Build, Configure, Use](https://www.decodingai.com/p/agentic-harness-system-design)**
A systems-design view of agentic harnesses, treating the scaffolding around the model (tools, configuration, permissions) as a first-class engineering artifact rather than glue code.

**[Forget job losses – most firms haven't switched AI on yet](https://techcentral.co.za/forget-job-losses-most-firms-havent-switched-ai-on-yet/283321)**
A useful counterweight to the doom headlines: real enterprise AI adoption is still shallow, and the gap between AI discourse and AI deployment remains enormous.

## 🛠️ Tools

**[I Maxed Out Fable 5 and Regretted It. Here's How I Actually Run It Now](https://thoughts.jock.pl/p/fable-5-efficiency-high-not-max-2026?publication_id=1540552&post_id=206194124)**
A hands-on account of running Claude Fable 5 at maximum reasoning effort and discovering it's mostly wasted tokens. The practical takeaway: "high" is the sweet spot for daily coding work, and knowing when to dial effort up beats leaving it pinned at max.

## 🧪 Productivity

**[Please stop the AI Confidence Theater](https://www.elenaverna.com/p/please-stop-the-ai-confidence-theater)**
Elena Verna calls out the performative certainty around AI strategy, leaders projecting confidence about tools and roadmaps nobody actually understands yet. Refreshingly honest take on admitting what we don't know.

## 📰 Other

**[What I Learned from Attending 23 Developer Events in 6 Months](https://blog.kilo.ai/p/23-events?publication_id=4363009&post_id=204711201&isFreemail=true&triedRedirect=true)**
Field notes from a half-year sprint through 23 developer events: what formats actually work, what developers respond to, and why smaller gatherings often beat the big-stage conferences.
