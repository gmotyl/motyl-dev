## TLDR;

Public trust in AI is low, impacting adoption and development. Meanwhile, Meta unveils an open 3D generation pipeline, World Labs introduces editable virtual spaces, Baidu releases new multimodal models, and Google DeepMind enhances robot coordination through graph neural networks.

## In-depth Summary

### Public Trust in AI

Recent reports from Edelman and Pew Research indicate widespread distrust and lack of excitement for AI in the U.S. and parts of Europe. This public sentiment can hinder AI's progress through slow adoption, stymied projects (like data center protests), and restrictive laws. The AI community needs to address these concerns by ensuring AI benefits everyone and by avoiding hype and fear-mongering, which have damaged public trust.

### Open 3D Generation Pipeline (Meta)

Meta has evolved its Segment Anything Model (SAM) into an open-weights suite for generating 3D objects. SAM 3 segments images and videos based on text input, outperforming most competitors in Meta's tests. SAM 3D converts these segments into 3D objects, with human judges preferring its outputs over other models for both objects and scenes. SAM 3D Body produces 3D human figures from images. This unified pipeline simplifies 3D model creation from images, driving innovations in user experiences like Facebook Marketplace.

### Generated, Editable Virtual Spaces (World Labs' Marble)

World Labs has launched Marble, a new model that generates persistent, editable, and reusable 3D virtual spaces from various inputs like text and images. Unlike previous models that created transient spaces, Marble's outputs can be exported and modified using its integrated editor, Chisel. Users can expand and combine spaces, alter styles, and download outputs in formats like Gaussian splats or meshes. This advancement aims to catalyze development in spatial AI, similar to how large language models boosted text processing.

### Baidu’s Multimodal Bids

Baidu has introduced two new models: Ernie-4.5-VL-28B-A3B-Thinking, a lightweight, open-weights vision-language model, and Ernie-5.0, a larger proprietary multimodal model. Ernie-4.5-VL-28B-A3B-Thinking excels in visual reasoning and document understanding, outperforming larger models like Gemini 2.5 Pro and GPT-5 in some tasks. Ernie-5.0 is designed as a natively multimodal model, trained on text, images, audio, and video concurrently, and reportedly performs comparably to Google Gemini 2.5 or OpenAI GPT-5. However, Ernie-5.0 has shown issues with tool calling. While Ernie-4.5-VL offers cost-effective visual reasoning and flexibility, Ernie-5.0's performance may not fully meet expectations, even with its innovative multimodal pretraining approach.
