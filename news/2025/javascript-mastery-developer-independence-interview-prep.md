## Wasted Years Learning JavaScript

**TLDR:** The author argues that jumping into JavaScript frameworks like React without a solid grasp of fundamental JavaScript concepts is a waste of time. True mastery comes from understanding the basics, DOM manipulation, asynchronous operations, and the client-server model first.

**Summary:** The video discussion emphasizes a common pitfall for modern developers: the rush to learn popular frameworks at the expense of foundational knowledge. This approach often leads to "tutorial hell," where developers can follow instructions but cannot build independently or solve problems effectively. The speaker suggests that a deep understanding of vanilla JavaScript, how it interacts with the DOM, and core asynchronous concepts like Promises and async/await are far more valuable in the long run. He makes a strong case that even frontend developers benefit immensely from understanding the full stack, as it provides context for their work and leads to better architectural decisions. The core message is a critique of a trend-driven learning process, advocating for a return to fundamentals to build a more resilient and versatile skill set. The author is avoiding the discussion of how the modern frameworks actually help in building complex applications and how they solve the problems of the past.

**Key takeaways:**
-   Prioritize learning fundamental JavaScript before moving to frameworks.
-   Focus on DOM manipulation, async concepts, and client-server dynamics.
-   Understanding the full stack is beneficial even for frontend specialists.
-   Avoid "tutorial hell" by building a strong foundational skill set.

## How to Prepare for Technical Job Interviews

**TLDR:** An 18-month job search revealed that interview struggles were due to recall under pressure, not a lack of knowledge. The solution was to use active recall techniques with flashcards, proactively ask recruiters for preparation topics, and focus on companies that value practical skills.

**Summary:** The author shares a personal and insightful story about their lengthy 18-month job search. Despite possessing the necessary development skills, they consistently failed technical interviews. The root cause wasn't a knowledge gap, but the inability to recall information effectively under the high-pressure conditions of an interview. The breakthrough came from adopting several key strategies. Firstly, they implemented active recall using flashcards, which forced their brain to retrieve information on demand, simulating the interview environment. Secondly, they started asking recruiters directly what topics to prepare for, which allowed for more focused and efficient studying. Finally, they shifted their job search strategy to target companies and roles that were a better fit for their skills and interview style. This article is a valuable reminder that technical interview performance is a skill in itself, separate from day-to-day development work, and that targeted preparation techniques can make a significant difference.

**Key takeaways:**
-   Interview struggles can be about recall under pressure, not just knowledge.
-   Use active recall techniques like flashcards to improve on-demand knowledge retrieval.
-   Proactively ask recruiters for a list of topics to focus your preparation.
-   Target companies whose interview process aligns with your strengths.
