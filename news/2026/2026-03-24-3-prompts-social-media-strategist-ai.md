## 3 Prompts Every Social Media Strategist Should Run Right Now

**TLDR:** A marketer reduced monthly content calendar work from 3 weeks to 20 minutes using 3 AI prompts. Prompt 1 collects business context and diagnoses broken social media. Prompt 2 finds trending questions and generates 20 viral content ideas. Prompt 3 packs ideas into a 30-day calendar with captions, formats, and testing plans.

**Summary:**

This is a practical AI workflow for social media strategists. A marketer used to spend three weeks building her monthly content calendar. Research, brainstorming, spreadsheet formatting, second-guessing every idea. Good work, but slow. And slow means half those ideas are stale before they ever get posted.

She added this workflow to her process. Same quality thinking, 20 minutes instead of three weeks. Two of her first five posts hit four-figure engagement for the first time in her career. She didn't stop being a strategist. She became a faster one.

This is the kind of workflow that separates professionals who get promoted from ones still doing everything by hand. The author took a 5-prompt content system, stripped it to three prompts, and built it so any marketer, content lead, or business owner can run it during a lunch break. No image uploads. No extra tools. You paste, you answer, you get a full month of content ready to schedule.

Prompt 1 collects your business context and diagnoses where your social media is broken. It asks about your business, audience, current content performance, and goals. The output identifies gaps in your strategy, content types that aren't working, and quick wins you can implement immediately.

Prompt 2 finds trending questions your audience is searching for right now and turns them into 20 viral content ideas. It analyzes your niche, identifies pain points, and generates specific content angles. Each idea includes a hook, main point, and call-to-action.

Prompt 3 packs those ideas into a 30-day calendar with captions, formats, and a testing plan. It assigns each idea to optimal posting days, suggests formats (carousel, video, text), and includes caption variations. The calendar includes a testing framework to track what works.

The whole thing runs inside one conversation thread. Each prompt builds on the last. Save the thread when you're done, and you've got a reusable content engine you can re-run every month with a fresh topic. Takes 15 to 30 minutes. Works in ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or Grok.

**Why do I care:**

As a developer who also handles technical content and social media, this workflow is valuable. The time savings are real. Three weeks to 20 minutes is a 95% reduction. That's not automation replacing work - that's automation eliminating drudgery so you can focus on strategy. The prompt structure is smart. Diagnose first, then generate ideas, then organize. Most people skip the diagnosis and jump to content generation. But if your strategy is broken, more content just amplifies the brokenness. The testing framework in Prompt 3 is crucial. Social media is experimental. You need to track what works and iterate. A calendar without testing is just a schedule of guesses. I'd recommend this to any developer building in public or managing technical content. The prompts are copy-paste ready. The workflow is platform-agnostic. Run it monthly. Track results. Refine based on what performs. For the cost of 20 minutes per month, you get a quarter's worth of content strategy.
